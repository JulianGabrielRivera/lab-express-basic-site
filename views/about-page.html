Ivar the Boneless was one of the most feared Vikings in history â€” and he couldn't even stand without somebody holding him up. In Viking tradition, someone like him would've been killed at birth, but Ivar was protected because he was the son of a powerful chief. His body was so frail that he had to be carried on a shield when he went into battle, but his mind was one of the sharpest around. This was the man who led the Great Viking Heathen Army on its conquest of England. In the late 9th century under Ivar, the Vikings terrorized the nation and conquered everything from Essex to Dublin. Ivar ushered in an era of Viking domination over Britain that wouldn't end until long after his death. And, as legend has it, he didn't have a single bone in his body.
